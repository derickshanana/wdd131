Section 01

Site Name

Zimbabwe Wild

zimbabwewild.com  ·  Proposed Domain

Zimbabwe Wild is a name that is short, memorable, and instantly communicates the subject matter — Zimbabwe's remarkable untamed landscapes and wildlife. The word Wild carries double meaning: it references wilderness and wild animals, but also conveys the excitement and wonder that visitors experience in Zimbabwe's national parks. The name works well as a brand across digital platforms and is easy to recall.

Optional domain: zimbabwewild.com  |  Alternate: zim-wild.org

Section 02

Site Purpose

Zimbabwe Wild is an informational website dedicated to showcasing Zimbabwe's national parks and wildlife reserves. It serves as a resource for travelers, wildlife enthusiasts, and conservation advocates who want to learn about Zimbabwe's natural heritage and plan meaningful visits.

The site will provide descriptive profiles of Zimbabwe's top national parks (Hwange, Mana Pools, Gonarezhou, Matobo Hills, and others), highlight the iconic and rare wildlife found in each park, and offer a practical trip-planning guide. Visitors can submit a trip inquiry form, save their park preferences using browser storage, and receive a personalized visit summary — all powered by JavaScript.

The site aims to bridge the gap in mainstream travel content by presenting Zimbabwe as a world-class wildlife destination, delivered with an authentic, locally informed perspective.

Section 03

Scenarios

The following questions represent the kinds of things a typical visitor — a traveler or wildlife enthusiast — might ask when visiting Zimbabwe Wild:

  • Which national park in Zimbabwe offers the best chance of seeing elephants up close?
  • What is the best time of year to visit Zimbabwe for a safari, and which parks should I prioritize?
  • How do I plan a trip to Mana Pools, and what activities are available there?
  • What endangered or rare animals can I see in Zimbabwe that I might not find elsewhere in Africa?

Section 05

Typography

Three fonts are used to create a distinctive, editorial identity that balances elegance with readability:

Font 1 — Playfair Display  ·  Headings & Display Text

Zimbabwe's Wild Heart

Usage: H1, H2, H3, section titles, park names, hero text. Weight 600 & 800. Provides an editorial, distinguished character that befits the subject's grandeur.

Font 2 — Source Sans 3  ·  Body & Paragraphs

Zimbabwe is home to over 350 mammal species, including the iconic African elephant, lion, leopard, Cape buffalo, and white rhinoceros — the "Big Five." Its national parks preserve some of the most pristine ecosystems on the African continent.

Usage: All body paragraphs, navigation links, form labels, list items. Weight 300, 400 & 600. Highly legible and optimised for screen reading at all sizes.

Font 3 — Courier Prime  ·  Labels, Metadata & Code

NATIONAL PARK  ·  EST. 1928  ·  HWANGE, ZIMBABWE

Usage: Section labels, captions, tags, dates, coordinates, form helper text. Weight 400 & 700. Adds an authentic field-notes aesthetic.

Section 06

Pages & Structure

The website will consist of at least three pages with consistent navigation and visual theme:

  • index.html — Home Hero banner, site introduction, featured park highlights, call-to-action to explore parks.
  • parks.html — Parks & Wildlife Dynamic park cards generated from a JavaScript array of objects. Filter and favorites features.
  • visit.html — Plan Your Visit HTML form for trip inquiry. LocalStorage saves preferences. JS renders a personalized summary.
  • references.html — References Unstyled page listing all image and text sources. Linked in the site footer.
